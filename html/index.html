Communication Concepts

Data communication is the exchange of digital or analog information between two or more devices through a transmission medium such as copper wire, fiber optic cable, radio waves, or satellite links.

A sender creates a message, a transmitter converts it into a signal, a channel carries the signal, and a receiver reconstructs the message for the destination.

Signals make communication possible because they convert information into electrical, optical, or wireless patterns that can travel across distance and be interpreted by another device.

Telegraph section

How the Telegraph Works

From key press to message

A telegraph sends messages by turning written language into controlled electrical pulses. The operator presses a telegraph key to close an electric circuit. When the circuit closes, current flows through the wire to a distant station and energizes an electromagnet.

The receiving electromagnet moves a sounder or marker, producing clicks or marks. Short and long pulses represent dots and dashes in Morse code, so the receiver can translate the pattern back into letters, words, and full messages.

  • Telegraph key controls the circuit.
  • Battery or power source sends current through the line.
  • Electromagnet at the receiver turns pulses into readable signals.
